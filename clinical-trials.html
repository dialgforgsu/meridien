Clinical Trials

Meridien Dermatology conducts clinical research through the Texas Dermatology Research Center (TXDRC), giving patients access to the latest investigational therapies under expert supervision.

About the Program

Texas Dermatology Research Center

The Texas Dermatology Research Center (TXDRC) is an academic research program affiliated with Meridien Dermatology. Under the direction of Dr. So Yeon Paek as Principal Investigator, TXDRC conducts Phase II–IV clinical trials investigating new and emerging therapies for inflammatory skin diseases, including hidradenitis suppurativa, psoriasis, atopic dermatitis, and other dermatological conditions.

Participation in a clinical trial may give patients access to cutting-edge treatments not yet commercially available — often at no cost. All trials are reviewed and approved by an independent Institutional Review Board (IRB) to ensure patient safety.

IRB-Approved Protocols

Every study is independently reviewed to protect patient rights and safety.

At No Cost to Participants

Study-related treatments, labs, and visits are provided free of charge during enrollment.

Active Clinical Studies

Hidradenitis Suppurativa

Phase III Biologic Therapy — HS

Investigating the efficacy and safety of a novel biologic agent targeting IL-17 in patients with moderate-to-severe hidradenitis suppurativa. Patients with Hurley Stage II or III who have had an inadequate response to systemic antibiotics may be eligible.

Ages 18–65 Stage II–III HS Phase III

Psoriasis

Plaque Psoriasis — Novel Oral Agent

A randomized, double-blind study evaluating a next-generation oral small-molecule therapy for moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis. Participants must have at least 10% body surface area involvement or significant disease affecting quality of life.

Ages 18+ Moderate–Severe Phase III
Enrolling
Atopic Dermatitis

Adult Atopic Dermatitis — Topical Study

Evaluating a next-generation non-steroidal topical treatment in adults with moderate atopic dermatitis. Patients who have had an inadequate response to over-the-counter therapies and prescription corticosteroids may qualify.

Ages 18+ Moderate AD Phase II/III

Getting Started

How to Participate

1

Contact Our Office

Call (972) 200-7446 or message through the patient portal to express your interest in clinical research. A coordinator will reach out to discuss open studies.

2

Screening Visit

You will come in for a free screening evaluation to determine if you meet the eligibility criteria for an open study. This visit has no cost to you.

3

Informed Consent & Enrollment

If eligible and you choose to enroll, you will review and sign an informed consent form. All study-related visits, labs, and medications are provided at no charge.

Interested?

Ask about clinical trials at your next visit

Our research coordinator is happy to discuss whether a current study might be right for you. Participation is always voluntary and you may withdraw at any time.
