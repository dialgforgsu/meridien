Condition Overview

Hidradenitis Suppurativa (HS)

Hidradenitis suppurativa is a chronic, inflammatory skin disease that affects hair follicles in areas where skin rubs together. HS causes painful lumps, abscesses, and tunnels (sinus tracts) under the skin that can drain and scar over time. Dr. Paek is one of the leading HS specialists in the Dallas area, offering the full spectrum of medical and surgical care.

Disease Stages — Hurley Classification
Stage I · Mild
Single Abscesses
One or more individual abscesses without sinus tracts or significant scarring.
Stage II · Moderate
Recurrent Lesions
Recurrent abscesses with sinus tract formation and scarring; separated by normal skin.
Stage III · Severe
Diffuse Involvement
Widespread interconnected tracts, multiple abscesses, and extensive scarring across the affected area.
Associated Conditions

HS is a systemic inflammatory condition. Patients have higher rates of:

Depression & Anxiety Arthropathies Type 2 Diabetes Inflammatory Bowel Disease PCOS Metabolic Syndrome
Lifestyle Modifications
Smoking Cessation Strongly linked to disease severity — quitting can significantly reduce flares.
Weight Management Reducing friction in skin folds can lessen symptoms in many patients.
Loose Clothing Minimizing friction and heat reduces irritation in affected areas.
Dietary Changes Some patients benefit from dairy or high-glycemic food reduction.

Surgical Program

MODES — Modified Deroofing

MODES (Modified Deroofing) is a minimally invasive surgical procedure developed for the treatment of hidradenitis suppurativa. Rather than removing large areas of tissue as in traditional wide excision, MODES precisely removes only the roof of sinus tracts and diseased tissue while preserving healthy surrounding skin.

Dr. Paek performs MODES at Meridien Dermatology as part of a comprehensive HS treatment program. The procedure can be performed in an office or surgical setting and is typically reserved for patients with Stage II or Stage III HS who have not responded adequately to medical management.

What to Expect
1

Consultation & Planning

Dr. Paek evaluates your HS stage, affected areas, and medical history to determine if MODES is appropriate and plan the procedure.

2

Local Anesthesia

The targeted area is numbed with local anesthesia so the procedure is comfortable. Most patients report minimal discomfort.

3

Deroofing

The roof of each sinus tract is carefully removed, exposing and removing the diseased tissue while leaving healthy skin intact.

4

Healing & Follow-up

The wound is left to heal by secondary intention. Recovery is typically faster than wide excision, with regular follow-up to monitor healing.

MODES vs. Wide Excision

Traditional Wide Excision
  • Removes large areas of skin and tissue
  • Longer recovery time
  • Requires skin grafting in some cases
  • More scarring
  • Higher surgical complexity
MODES
  • Preserves healthy surrounding skin
  • Faster healing and recovery
  • No grafting typically required
  • Reduced scarring
  • Can be done in-office

MODES is most effective as part of a comprehensive treatment plan that includes ongoing medical management. Dr. Paek will work with you to combine the procedure with appropriate systemic therapies for the best long-term outcomes.
