Hidradenitis Suppurativa & MODES

Hidradenitis suppurativa (HS) is a chronic, debilitating inflammatory skin condition that affects hair follicles in body folds — underarms, groin, thighs, and buttocks. It causes painful nodules, abscesses, and tunnels under the skin that can lead to significant scarring. Dr. Paek is one of the leading HS specialists in the Dallas area.

Disease Stages (Hurley Classification)
Stage I
Mild
Single or multiple abscesses without sinus tracts or scarring.
Stage II
Moderate
Recurrent abscesses with sinus tracts and scarring; lesions separated by normal skin.
Stage III
Severe
Diffuse involvement with interconnected tunnels and extensive scarring.

Treatment Approach

Medical
  • Benzoyl peroxide & CLn wash
  • Topical clindamycin
  • Oral antibiotics
  • Hormonal therapy
  • Metformin, acitretin
Advanced
  • Adalimumab (Humira®)
  • Secukinumab
  • Bimekizumab
  • Laser therapy
  • Intralesional steroids
Surgical Program

MODES — Modified Deroofing

MODES is a specialized surgical procedure for HS in which the roof of sinus tracts is carefully removed while healthy surrounding skin is preserved. Compared to wide excision, MODES offers faster healing, better cosmetic outcomes, and reduced recurrence. Dr. Paek performs MODES as part of Meridien's comprehensive HS program.

Clinical Trials

Visit TXDRC.com

Meridien Dermatology is affiliated with the Texas Dermatology Research Center (TXDRC), a clinical research center located at our same address. Dr. Paek serves as Principal Investigator for multiple IRB-approved clinical trials, giving patients access to investigational treatments before FDA approval — at no cost to participants.

Phase 3 · Topical

Topical Treatment Study

Randomized, double-blind, controlled study evaluating the efficacy and safety of a topical cream for hidradenitis suppurativa.

Ages 12+  ·  Mild to Moderate HS
Phase 3 · Oral

Oral Medication Study

Phase 3 trial of an oral agent for moderate-to-severe HS, including a long-term extension component for continued monitoring.

Adults  ·  Moderate to Severe HS
Phase 3 · Injectable

Subcutaneous Injection Study

Evaluating the safety and efficacy of a subcutaneous injectable agent for adults with moderate-to-severe hidradenitis suppurativa.

Adults  ·  Moderate to Severe HS
Phase 3 · Adolescent

Pediatric & Adolescent Study

Open-label study evaluating pharmacokinetics and safety of a subcutaneous injection in adolescents with hidradenitis suppurativa.

Ages 12–17  ·  HS
